The Abilene dataset is a real-world network traffic dataset collected from the Abilene backbone network in the United States. It consists of traffic flow measurements between 12 nodes, resulting in 144 origin-destination traffic flows. The data captures hourly traffic volumes in bytes, exhibiting strong periodicity, diurnal patterns, and occasional anomalous spikes caused by network failures, DDoS attacks, or sudden traffic bursts. The task is point anomaly detection: identifying individual time steps where traffic behavior deviates significantly from normal patterns.
